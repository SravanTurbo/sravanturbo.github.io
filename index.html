Writing Work

There's a pattern to how I approach problems that I only noticed in hindsight. At Transin I went on-ground to a branch office to understand why field agents weren't entering data — the team assumed it was a compliance issue, the reality was that the tool gave them nothing back. At Whitehat Jr. I joined a struggling team as a developer before taking technical charge, because I wanted to experience the incidents before I tried to fix them. At GoHeartBeat I designed a compliance architecture that no client had asked for yet, because I could see the constraint coming. The pattern, if there is one: go closer to the problem than the problem requires.

Ten years of backend systems. This site is where I write about the decisions — the reasoning that doesn't fit in a resume bullet, the wrong turns before the right answer, the things I'd have wanted to read earlier.
