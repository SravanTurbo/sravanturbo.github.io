About

I'm a principal backend engineer focused on distributed systems, data pipelines, and the operational side of running software at scale. I've spent most of my career at the intersection of platform engineering and product — building the infrastructure that product teams rely on without thinking about it.

I write here to think through decisions I've made or watched others make. Most posts start as internal documents — design reviews, postmortems, migration proposals — that I've stripped of company-specific detail and turned into something I'd have wanted to read earlier in my career. The goal is to be honest about tradeoffs, not to document the happy path.

Outside work I think about type systems, read about urban planning, and have opinions about coffee that aren't worth publishing.
