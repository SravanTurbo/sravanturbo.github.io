About

Principal backend engineer, ten years in, based in Hyderabad. The work has mostly lived at the intersection of distributed systems and product — building the infrastructure that lets a product do something it couldn't do before, or diagnosing why it stopped doing something it used to.

The career has been a series of bets that compound oddly. Early on at Eclerx I introduced Kafka into a security monitoring pipeline because logs were being dropped when Elasticsearch nodes went down — nobody asked me to, it just seemed like the right call. At Toppr I diagnosed a Python GIL bottleneck as the root cause of a scaling failure nobody had correctly named, led the migration to Go, and watched compute drop from ~300 cores to ~30. At Whitehat Jr. I walked in on a gutted team and a platform that was paging engineers multiple times a night — turned out 90% of the incidents traced back to reactive auto-scaling lag during peak class hours, one fixable root cause. At GoHeartBeat I designed a compliance-first multi-tenant architecture before any client asked for it, which directly closed the first enterprise contract.

The through-line is diagnosis before fixing. I'm slow to patch symptoms. Most of the interesting engineering work I've done started with going a level deeper than the immediate problem — visiting a branch office to understand why agents weren't entering data, joining a struggling team as a developer before taking technical charge, writing incident reports in the common channel so the whole org could learn from them, not just the team on call.

This site is a working notebook. The posts are decision narratives — the reasoning behind calls I've made in production, the tradeoffs that don't fit in a resume bullet, the things I'd have wanted to read earlier. The goal is to be honest about what worked and what didn't, not to document the happy path.
