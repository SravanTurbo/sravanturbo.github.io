The right tool put the right people to work.

2025

The previous post ended with finding the semantic layer — a way to model a company's data so the knowledge doesn't disappear when people move on.

But finding the right tool is only half the story.

At this point I was doing everything. Modeling the client's data, writing the queries, building the backend, managing infrastructure. Every time a stakeholder had a new question, it came to me. I'd go back to the client's engineering team, understand whatever part of the database I hadn't mapped yet, write the query, return with an answer.

The product guy had something I didn't. He understood the business — what clients were trying to measure, why certain numbers mattered, what questions were likely coming next. But he couldn't write SQL. So that understanding stayed in conversations. It never made it into the data.

I gave him access to the CubeJS query layer once the first client's data was modeled. Not the modeling itself — just the ability to query what was already there.

He didn't need SQL. He picked a measure, applied a dimension, filtered by time. The system handled the translation underneath. What he needed to understand was the model, not the syntax. And the only way to query the model was to understand it.

He started exploring. Not just answering what stakeholders asked but going wider. He started correlating things, finding patterns adjacent to the original question, coming back with more than what was asked for. The friction of SQL had been sitting between him and the actual thinking he was capable of doing.

By the second client, he was querying the first client's data on his own. By the third, he was handling the modeling and querying for both. I jumped in when something was complex. Everything else was his.

The semantic layer did what I designed it to do. But the real outcome wasn't technical.

He was doing the work he was actually good at. I was free to build instead of spending my days answering questions. The same decision put both of us in the right place.
